Progress Update — April 24, 2026 at 5:06 PM

WIN OF THE DAY:
The system got sharper on three fronts today: low-stock alerts carry a full paper trail in the daily report, stock search now finds anything by serial number or UPC, and item details become editable right where any item is opened.

WHAT'S BETTER NOW:
- Every order completion now shows how many units are left warehouse-wide under each SKU, so the picker knows right away when a part is running dry.
- The daily report's floor block lists today's depletions with the full story: which SKU, how many went out, which order took them, and at what time — one tap on the order number pulls up the full summary.
- Stock search used to miss bikes whose serial was on the tag but whose SKU you didn't remember — now it finds them by serial, by UPC barcode, by model name, or by compatible-models description.
- Scratch-and-dent bikes show their physical serial in the big header during double-check, so pickers can match the tag without guessing the SKU.
- Opening a bike or part now has a clean "Details" card with Serial, Price, Condition, Notes, and PDF link — same pretty look as before, but click the pencil and it edits in place. Leaving with unsaved changes now asks before throwing them away.
- All non-S/D bikes in the catalog were marked "New" in bulk (565 units), and four pedals that had been mistakenly tagged as bikes got moved back to parts.

COMING UP NEXT:
- Orders screen visual rework — carrier logos, glass styling, cleaner hierarchy.
- Bike / Part / Unknown selector when registering a new SKU, so the system never has to guess.

17 shipped today / 6 on the roadmap (mostly quality-of-life tweaks and one larger visual rework).
