Progress Update — April 3, 2026 at 5:30 PM

WIN OF THE DAY:
Shipping labels now show bikes and parts as separate counts, customers can have multiple addresses, and cycle counts are now tracked in the system with a full history screen.

WHAT'S BETTER NOW:
- Labels used to say "UNITS: 18" with no distinction — now they show "BIKES: 15" and "PARTS: 3" separately, matching how the warehouse actually counts.
- Customers were limited to one address. Now the system remembers every address you ship to — just tap the address field and pick from the list. New addresses save automatically when you print.
- Parts used to inherit bike measurements (54 inches, 45 lbs) which made weight calculations wrong. Now parts start at zero and bikes keep their real defaults.
- You can now edit the weight of individual parts right below the label preview instead of hunting for the item in stock.
- FedEx orders no longer add 40 lbs per pallet to the weight — they show product weight only. A purple tag marks them in the order list.
- Cycle counts are now saved to the system instead of disappearing when you close the app. A new history screen shows all past audits with expected vs actual counts and discrepancies.
- Items verified in a cycle count within the last two months show a green indicator on their stock card — so you can see at a glance what's been recently audited.
- The app was making duplicate calls on every page load — cut those in half, so everything loads faster.
- Editing an item from the verification screen now opens the full item view (with photo and distribution) instead of the old cramped popup.
- The keyboard no longer pops up when opening an order for verification.
- Dimension and weight badges now show on stock cards for bikes. Parts with no measurements stay clean.
